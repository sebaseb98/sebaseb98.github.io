Radon Transform

The Radon transform is a mathematical transformation used in tomography to reconstruct an image from its projections.

What is the Radon Transform?

The Radon transform is named after the Austrian mathematician Johann Radon. It is a mathematical operation that maps functions defined on a plane to functions defined on a line.

In medical imaging, such as Computed Tomography (CT) scans, the Radon transform is used to reconstruct images of the internal structures of the body from a series of X-ray projections.

Applications

Some common applications of the Radon transform include:

  • Medical imaging (CT scans)
  • Non-destructive testing
  • Seismology
  • Remote sensing
